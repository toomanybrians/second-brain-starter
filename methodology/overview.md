# What is a second brain?

A second brain is a personal AI knowledge system: a folder of text files on your computer that your AI reads at the start of every conversation. Unlike chat history (which is ephemeral) or memory features (which are shallow), a second brain gives your AI deep, persistent, structured access to everything you know, think, and care about.

## Why it works

When you use AI without a second brain, every conversation starts from zero. You re-explain your role, your context, your preferences, your projects. The AI is smart but amnesiac.

With a second brain, the AI starts every conversation already knowing who you are, what you're working on, how you like to communicate, what your positions are, what decisions you've made, and what you're thinking about. It goes from a capable stranger to a cognitive partner that compounds over time.

## What it actually is (technically)

- A folder on your computer (or in cloud storage)
- Containing markdown (.md) text files organized by purpose
- With a master instruction file (CLAUDE.md) that tells any AI how to navigate the system
- Managed via git for version history (optional but recommended)

That's it. No special software, no database, no app to install. Just files.

## The components

### 1. Master instruction file (CLAUDE.md)
The most important file. It tells your AI: here's who I am, here's how the system works, here's what to read first, here's how to behave. Any AI that reads this file can immediately operate your knowledge system.

### 2. Identity layer (me/)
Who you are: your role, your domain expertise, your communication preferences, where your thinking is right now, how you write. This is what makes the AI feel like it knows you.

### 3. Knowledge layer (work/, reference/, research/)
Your actual knowledge: what you're working on, what you've read, what you've published, what you know about your domain. Organized by how you use it, not how a librarian would categorize it.

### 4. Idea capture (ideas/)
Raw seeds, connections you've noticed, things you don't want to lose. Not everything needs a home on day one. This is the scratchpad that feeds everything else.

### 5. Processing layer (skills/)
Workflows your AI follows: how to process a meeting transcript, how to handle an article you've read, how to do a weekly review. These are like SOPs for your AI, expressed as markdown files.

### 6. Active work (inbox/, tasks/, drafts/)
The operational layer: things coming in, things to do, things in progress.

## The key insight: integration, not filing

Most knowledge management fails because it's organized forgetting. You file something, feel productive, and never look at it again.

A second brain works differently. When new knowledge arrives, your AI doesn't just file it somewhere: it asks "what does this change? What does it connect to? Which of your current arguments does this strengthen or challenge?" Every piece of knowledge gets wired into your active thinking.

This is the difference between a filing cabinet and a brain. Filing cabinets store. Brains connect.

## What compounds

After a few weeks of use:
- Your AI knows your positions on key topics and can argue them
- Meeting notes automatically update your project context and task list
- Articles you read get wired into your active arguments
- Your writing voice is captured: the AI writes like you, not like a generic assistant
- Weekly reviews surface connections you missed
- New information gets contextualized against everything you already know

The system gets more valuable every day because knowledge compounds when it's connected.

## Who this is for

Anyone who does knowledge work: executives, strategists, researchers, writers, consultants, engineers, product managers. If your job involves thinking, deciding, writing, or advising, a second brain makes you dramatically better at it.

You don't need to be technical. If you can create a folder and edit a text file, you can build a second brain. The AI handles the rest.
